EmailJS Setup Guide

Connect your website forms to afaqalhind@gmail.com

What this does: When a visitor submits a form on your website (Home quick-enquiry or Contact page), EmailJS sends all their details directly to afaqalhind@gmail.com — no backend server needed. The free plan allows 200 emails/month.

Files that use EmailJS: index.html pages/contact.html js/emailjs-handler.js
1

Create a free EmailJS account

Go to https://www.emailjs.com and sign up for a free account. The free plan gives you 200 emails per month which is more than enough to start.

2

Connect your Gmail account

In your EmailJS dashboard:

→ Click Email Services in the left sidebar

→ Click Add New Service

→ Choose Gmail

→ Click Connect Account and log in with afaqalhind@gmail.com

→ Give it a name like Afaq Al Hind Gmail and click Create Service

→ Copy the Service ID shown (looks like: service_abc1234)

⚠️ Important: Make sure you are logged into the correct Gmail account (afaqalhind@gmail.com) in your browser before clicking Connect Account.
3

Create an Email Template

→ Click Email Templates in the left sidebar

→ Click Create New Template

→ Set the To Email field to: afaqalhind@gmail.com

→ Set the Subject to:

New Enquiry — {{service}} (from {{from_name}})

→ In the Content / Body area, paste this exactly:

New enquiry received from the Afaq Al-Hind website. ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ ENQUIRY DETAILS ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ Name: {{from_name}} Phone / WhatsApp: {{phone}} Email: {{reply_to}} Nationality: {{nationality}} Service Required: {{service}} Preferred Contact: {{contact_method}} Message: {{message}} ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ Sent from afaqalhind.ae website forms

→ Click Save then copy the Template ID (looks like: template_xyz7890)

4

Get your Public Key

→ Click your profile icon (top right) → Account

→ Under the General tab, find Public Key

→ Copy it (looks like: AbCdEfGhIjKlMnOpQr)

5

Paste your keys into the website

Open the file js/emailjs-handler.js in any text editor (Notepad, VS Code, etc.) and replace the placeholder values at the top of the file:

// Replace these three values with your actual EmailJS credentials: const EMAILJS_SERVICE_ID = 'service_abc1234'; // ← paste your Service ID here const EMAILJS_TEMPLATE_ID = 'template_xyz7890'; // ← paste your Template ID here const EMAILJS_PUBLIC_KEY = 'AbCdEfGhIjKlMnOp'; // ← paste your Public Key here

→ Save the file. That's it — both forms are now live!

💡 Tip: These are safe to put in your website code. The Public Key is designed to be used client-side. Never share your Private Key (which EmailJS also has — just don't use that one).
6

Test it

→ Open index.html in your browser

→ Fill in the Quick Enquiry form at the bottom and submit

→ Check afaqalhind@gmail.com inbox — you should receive the email within seconds

→ Also test the full form on pages/contact.html

Not receiving emails? Check your Gmail Spam folder. Also verify that your EmailJS Service status shows Connected in the dashboard.

✅ Setup Complete!

Every form submission on your website will now be delivered directly to afaqalhind@gmail.com with all client details — name, phone, service needed, message and more.
